## 🎯 Learning Objectives

*By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:*

- Name the four major Forex sessions and their approximate trading hours
- Explain what **session overlaps** are and why they matter
- Understand how volatility and liquidity change across the 24-hour cycle
- Identify which sessions best match their own schedule and strategies

---

## 📖 LESSON CONTENT

### SECTION 1 — A 24-Hour Market with a Rhythm

Forex trades 24 hours a day, 5 days a week — but it does **not** behave the same way at all hours.

The market is driven by the opening hours of major financial centres:

- Sydney (Oceania)
- Tokyo (Asia)
- London (Europe)
- New York (North America)

When each region wakes up, its banks, funds, and corporations become active — bringing liquidity and movement. When it sleeps, activity dries up.

---

### SECTION 2 — The Four Major Sessions (in UTC)

To avoid time zone confusion, we describe sessions in **UTC**:

- **Sydney**: 22:00 – 07:00  
- **Tokyo**: 00:00 – 09:00  
- **London**: 07:00 – 16:00  
- **New York**: 12:00 – 21:00

The key feature is the **overlaps**:

- London + New York overlap (12:00 – 16:00 UTC)
- Asia overlaps briefly with Europe as London opens

These overlaps are where:

- Liquidity is deepest
- Spreads are tightest
- Intraday volatility is highest

### SECTION 3 — Session Personalities

Each session has its own “mood”:

- **Sydney / Early Asia**
  - Low volume
  - Often range-bound and slow

- **Tokyo (Asian Session)**
  - Most active in JPY and AUD pairs (e.g., USD/JPY, AUD/JPY, AUD/USD)
  - Often defines the early high/low for the day

- **London**
  - High volume, tight spreads
  - Most active in EUR and GBP pairs
  - Many key European and UK economic releases

- **New York**
  - Strong in USD pairs
  - Early New York overlaps with London — often the most volatile window of the day
  - US news (NFP, CPI, FOMC) can cause major moves

---

### SECTION 4 — Overlaps: Where Energy Concentrates

The **London–New York overlap** is effectively the market’s “prime time”:

- Both European and US desks are active
- Order books are deepest
- Many intraday trends begin or accelerate here

By contrast, the quiet handover from late New York to early Sydney often produces:

- Low volume
- Choppy, erratic moves
- Wider spreads — especially in crosses and exotics

### SECTION 5 — Matching Sessions to Your Life

You have a life outside trading — a job, studies, family. Rather than fighting that, you design your trading around it.

Examples:

- **European-based trader with a 9–5 job:**
  - Pre-work hour overlaps London open
  - Focus on EUR/USD, GBP/USD, EUR/GBP

- **Asia-based trader:**
  - Evening hours fall into Tokyo session
  - Focus on USD/JPY, AUD/JPY, AUD/USD

- **US-based trader:**
  - Morning window can catch the tail-end of London and the start of New York
  - Focus on USD majors (EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY)

In later units, you’ll define:

- Which pairs you specialise in
- Which sessions you commit to trading
- Which hours you deliberately avoid (e.g., low-liquidity chop or major news spikes)

---

## ✅ KEY TAKEAWAYS

- Forex is open 24/5, but **sessions and overlaps** determine when it’s truly liquid and volatile
- The four major sessions are **Sydney, Tokyo, London, New York**
- **London–New York overlap** is the most important window for many traders
- Different pairs are most active in different sessions
- You design your trading schedule around sessions that fit both your **strategy** and your **life**
