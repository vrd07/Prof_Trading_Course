## 🎯 Learning Objectives

*By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:*
- Read and interpret a currency pair quote
- Distinguish base vs quote currency
- Classify majors vs minors vs exotics and understand why it matters

---

## 📖 LESSON CONTENT

### SECTION 1 — The Language of Forex

Forex is expressed in **pairs** because every trade is a relationship between two currencies.

---

### SECTION 2 — Anatomy of a Currency Pair

```
EUR / USD
Base  Quote
```

If EUR/USD = 1.0850, then **1 EUR costs 1.0850 USD**.

<AnimationPlaceholder
  title="Pair Anatomy Dissection"
  takeaway="A pair is a ratio: left is what you’re measuring, right is the ruler."
/>

---

### SECTION 3 — Currency Codes

Most of your trading will involve a small set of major currencies (USD, EUR, GBP, JPY, CHF, AUD, NZD, CAD).

---

### SECTION 4 — The Three Categories of Pairs

- **Majors**: include USD, tightest spreads
- **Minors / crosses**: no USD, slightly wider spreads
- **Exotics**: emerging-market currency involved, wide spreads + lower liquidity

<AnimationPlaceholder
  title="The Pairs Classification Map"
  takeaway="Pair selection is a liquidity + cost decision. Majors are the most beginner-friendly."
/>

---

### SECTION 5 — Reading a Live Quote in Full

If you see:

```
EUR/USD    1.0848 / 1.0851
              BID     ASK
```

You **sell at BID** and **buy at ASK**. The spread is your cost of doing business.

---

### SECTION 6 — Why Pair Selection is Strategic

Start with **one pair**, ideally **EUR/USD**, and learn its behavior deeply.

<AnimationPlaceholder
  title="The Pair Selector"
  takeaway="There’s no universally best pair — the right one depends on your strategy and trading session."
/>

---

## ✅ KEY TAKEAWAYS

- Base currency is left; quote currency is right
- Majors are cheapest and most liquid
- Spread is a real cost you must plan for
