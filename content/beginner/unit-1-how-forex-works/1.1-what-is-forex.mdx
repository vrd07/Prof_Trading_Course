## 🎯 Learning Objectives

*By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:*
- Explain what the Forex market is in simple terms
- Understand why currencies have changing values
- Identify Forex as the largest financial market in the world
- Understand the basic mechanic of making money from currency price changes

---

## 📖 LESSON CONTENT

### SECTION 1 — The Simple Truth About Money

You've dealt with Forex before — even if you didn't know it.

If you've ever travelled abroad and exchanged your home currency for another country's currency at an airport kiosk, you participated in the foreign exchange market. You handed over one currency and received another at a specific rate.

But here's the key insight most people miss:

**That rate is not fixed. It changes every single second.**

---

### SECTION 2 — What Exactly is the Forex Market?

Forex stands for **Foreign Exchange**. It is the global marketplace where currencies are bought and sold.

Unlike the stock market — which has a central exchange — Forex has no physical location. It is a **decentralised, over-the-counter (OTC) market**.

### SECTION 3 — Why Do Currency Values Change?

Currency values move because global participants constantly compare two economies and decide where capital should flow.

The main forces that drive currency values:

| Driver | How it Affects Currency |
|---|---|
| **Interest Rates** | Higher rates attract foreign capital → currency strengthens |
| **Economic Growth (GDP)** | Strong growth signals → currency demand rises |
| **Inflation** | High inflation erodes purchasing power → currency weakens |
| **Political Stability** | Instability creates uncertainty → investors exit the currency |
| **Trade Balance** | Export surplus increases demand for domestic currency |
| **Market Sentiment** | Risk-on boosts some currencies; risk-off boosts safe havens |

---

### SECTION 4 — How Do Traders Actually Make Money?

Every Forex trade involves **two simultaneous actions**: you buy one currency and sell another.

### SECTION 5 — Who Can Trade Forex?

Anyone can access the market today through brokers — but consistent profitability is rare because most people trade without education, structure, and emotional discipline.

## ✅ KEY TAKEAWAYS

- Forex is the global market for buying and selling currencies — decentralised and huge
- Currency values change because they reflect economic reality and capital flows
- Every trade is a pair: buy one currency, sell another
- You can profit in both directions (long or short)
